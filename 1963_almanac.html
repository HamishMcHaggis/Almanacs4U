The year in culture

What topped the charts, won the big prize, and entered the language in 1963.

Word of the year
Beatlemania

The term was coined in 1963 as the Beatles took over Britain; a promoter is said to have used it in Glasgow that October.

Cost of living
Pounds, shillings, pence

Money was still £sd, with decimal currency three years away. Indicative figures: the new EH Holden sold for around £1,000 and the basic weekly wage was about £15.

Average house price
£2,840

That was the UK average. In the US the median was about $19,300. Reliable Australian capital-city data begins around 1970 (Sydney's median was $18,700 then), so early-1960s figures are sparse. Indicative.

Who was in charge
Robert Menzies

Menzies led Australia. John F. Kennedy held the White House, two months from Dallas, and Harold Macmillan Number 10 in his final weeks. Queen Elizabeth II reigned; Pope Paul VI, newly elected that June, led the Church.

Champions of the year
Geelong

Geelong won the VFL premiership and Gatum Gatum took the Melbourne Cup. In Europe, Tottenham Hotspur became the first British club to win a European trophy.

Signs & symbols

The almanac's older trade: the traditional markers tied to the year and to September 24. Folklore rather than science, and offered in that spirit.

Western zodiac
Libra

The Scales, an air sign ruled by Venus. September 24 falls in early Libra, just past the Virgo cusp.

Chinese zodiac
Water Rabbit

The lunar year Gui Mao began on 25 January 1963. Gentle and diplomatic, drawn to calm and harmony.

Birthstone
Sapphire

September's stone, a blue corundum tied to wisdom and loyalty.

Birth flowers
Aster

With morning glory, the traditional blooms of September.

Lucky numbers
6 · 9

Traditionally linked to Libra; the Rabbit adds 3, 4 and 9.

Lucky day & colours
Friday

Venus's day. Blue and pink are the Libra colours.

Numerology
6

September 24 reduces to 6, the number tied to Venus and Libra. The digits of 1963 reduce to 1.

That day
Tuesday

September 24, 1963 was a Tuesday, the 267th day of the year, at the turn into autumn.

Generation

A late Baby Boomer (1946 to 1964), born near the cusp of Generation X.

Life path number

Moon that night

Roughly six nights past the new moon, 24 September 1963 rose under a waxing crescent climbing toward first quarter.
